About Me

There are few professions in the world that can truly say there are no limits. Some may even say these professions are “out of this world”. For me, this profession is the world of computer science engineering. Currently, I am exploring the multiple classes and clubs at Dublin High School that may relate to this field. There are few professions that truly test one’s mathematical, science, and engineering abilities all at the same time, however, computer science engineering is one of them.

Where did my interest begin?

My interest in computer science started when I was around nine years old. My brother was taking AP Computer Science Principles in high school. One day, he decided to show me a project he was working on. It was a program that would display a number based on what number the user puts up in the webcam. For example, if the user put two fingers up, the program would display a "2". Thinking about it now, it wasn't the hardest program ever to create. It most likely took a few hours at most. However, I had no way of knowing this as a child.

What does it take to be an engineer?

In the realm of engineering, cooperation, critical thinking, and mathematical ability are some of the most important characteristics an engineer must have. Throughout high school so far, I have been able to accomplish multiple things that have been demonstrative of my passion for engineering. I am currently in Gael Force Robotics, have attended multiple guest speaker events under the topic of engineering, and have even structured my high school electives around engineering. Engineering is dependent on math and science as well, which is why I have participated in multiple science competitions and even a math competition. I am currently two years ahead of my class in math, and have maintained a near-perfect score in three years of science. In the future, I plan on joining more engineering clubs, taking more engineering classes, and joining the competitive team for Gael Force Robotics. To gain the communication and collaboration skills an engineer requires, I also plan on joining Dublin Key Club and possibly Teens for Tutors.

My experiences have taught me important life lessons. I have learned how important it is to cooperate and become a leader. I have also learned the importance of perseverance and striving for a better self. By the end of it, I hope to accomplish my goals and achieve everything I have set.
